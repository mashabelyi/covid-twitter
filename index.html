#COVID Twitter Dashboard

The Coronavirus pandmemic hit the United States in early spring, 2020. As common in times of crisis, millions of Americans took to Twitter to share and discuss information about Covid19.

We analyzed tweets originating in the United States1 between March 1 and March 30 to track how the conversation evolved over time. Scroll down to explore how the conversation evolved over time.

How much did different states tweet about COVID-19 in March? Hover over a state to see how many tweets mentioned COVID-19 for each date in the month of March.

How do we feel about Coronavirus? Twitter users expressed a wide range of emotions about Covid19 in March.

The interactive map below reflects daily average sentiment scores in USA for every day in March. Evidently, the shelter in place orders that came into effect in the middle of the month correlated with a systematic shift in sentiment across the country.

Select retweets to find out what kind of content users tend to retweet more.
Vader sentiment analysis tool was used to score each tweet on a sentiment scale from -1 to 1.
What did people talk about? Conversations on Twitter are often politically charged. Political discourse on social media is often polarized and prone to misinformation, which may have harmful effects in the public sphere2. Who plays a role in facilitating these conversations?

Looking at frequently occuring sequences of hashtags used when discussing COVID-19, we discovered that select accounts tend to re-use polarized hashtags multiple times over a short period of time.
